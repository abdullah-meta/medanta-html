High Dependency Unit

In situations when there is high patient flow in recovery units with a greater number of patients in need of the ICU, the relatively stable patients are transferred to the HDU instead of being sent back to the ward, as they would still need close monitoring. The High Dependency Unit is functionally a step-down ICU to care for patients until bodily functions are stabilised. Even if the patient-nurse ratio is 2:1, instead of 1:1 as in the ICU, all the facilities of a full state of the art ICU are at-the- ready should the patient suddenly become critical again.
