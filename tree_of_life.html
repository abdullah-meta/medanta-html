Dedicated to Life

Watch the story behind the Tree of Life

The philosophy of the Tree of Life

The age-old tradition of wish-making by tying a red thread on a tree spans across many religions. From the Chintpurni Temple in Himachal Pradesh to Ajmer Sharif Darga in Rajasthan, people of all faiths observe this tradition.

The tree of life symbolizes the preservation of all forms of life. This tree is a foundation stone and is placed here to fortify Medanta as a healing temple.

We want to cultivate a healing culture driven by the integration of modern medicine, traditional practices and cutting-edge technology.

It is our resolve to respect and save every life that comes to Medanta.

Why The Tree of Life

The Vedas and Puranas

In the Vedas and Puranas (Hindu texts), the tree symbolizes eternal life. It is often referred to as the Ashwath Vriksha.

The banyan tree

A temple of Healing

A Strong Purpose

We seek to achieve for all who come here wellness, good health and contentment. It is our primary purpose to respect and save every life that comes to Medanta.

Deep Rooted Inspiration

Inspired from Sushruta, a surgeon and teacher of Ayurveda in the 6th Century BC as well as the Charaka Samhita (3rd Century BC), an Ayurvedic text on internal medicine. We seek to honour traditional medicine, combining it with new age technologies to provide healing with compassion and commitment.

Stemming from Science

Scientific papers presented by prominent institutions prove that prayers promote rapid recovery. We deeply believe that your prayers are important in the healing process.
